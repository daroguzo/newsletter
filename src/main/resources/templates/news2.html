Minneapolis Residents Won't Call
Cops On Homeless Camp In Park

By Shannon Cain
JULY 30, 2020 — 9:41PM

A Minneapolis neighborhood has been overrun by a homeless encampment that’s grown to as many as 300 people — amid an agreement among progressive white residents to not call the police, according to a new report. The tent city has popped up in a park in Powderhorn Park — a diverse community of about 9,000 that’s blocks away from where George Floyd was killed last month. In light of Floyd’s police-involved death, residents there have vowed to avoid calling the cops, feeling that doing so could put people of color in danger. Some citizens have declared, "I will not use the police's security services for the safety of the black neighbor." There was also a 'stop calling police' campaign placard across Minneapolis. In view of popular public views, the city decided to pass a bill to reform the police organization and establish a new “Social Safety and Violence Prevention (Safety Bureau)” instead. Officials play the role that the police have played. The city council had taken some of the allegations that the police should be abolished. The bill, which passed the city council, will be submitted to the referendum in November after being reviewed by the city policy committee.
